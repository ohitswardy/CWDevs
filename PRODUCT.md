# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

SMEs across all industries and regions — business owners, founders, and decision-makers evaluating a systems partner. They arrive via referral, search, or outreach. They're buying on three signals simultaneously: trust (does this team feel credible?), spec (can they actually build what we need?), and case studies (have they done it before?). Tech sophistication varies widely — some know exactly what they want built, others are still defining the problem. The site must work for both without condescending to either.

## Product Purpose

CW Devs is a digital systems engineering firm. The site exists to convert a skeptical SME visitor into a client inquiry. Every section — services, case studies, process, tech stack, testimonials — exists to reduce friction on that one conversion. Success is a prospective client reaching out to start an engagement.

## Brand Personality

Intentional, Accurate, Ambitious.

Voice: Direct and confident without arrogance. Speaks in outcomes, not features. Doesn't oversell or hedge. Shows instead of tells. The copy and design both do real work — nothing decorative that isn't also persuasive.

Tone: Peer-level. Not a vendor pitching up, not an agency pitching down. A capable partner who knows what they're doing and trusts the client to recognize it.

## Anti-references

- **Sloppy**: poor spacing, inconsistent alignment, lazy typography, visual noise without purpose
- **Weak**: soft palettes, excessive whitespace without tension, passive copy, hedging language, anything that reads as unsure of itself
- **Generic dev agency clichés**: rocket emojis, "let's build something amazing together", identical icon-grid service cards, stock photo team shots, hero sections that could belong to any agency
- **Overly decorative motion**: animations that run for their own sake with no narrative or persuasive purpose
- **Enterprise gray**: cold corporate neutrals with no edge or conviction

## Design Principles

1. **Every element earns its place.** Nothing decorative that isn't also persuasive. If a visual element doesn't help the visitor trust, understand, or act — cut it.
2. **Confidence without volume.** Intentional and accurate means precision, not loudness. The design should feel like a firm handshake, not a pitch deck.
3. **Show the work, not the hype.** Case studies, system previews, and real outputs carry more weight than superlatives. Make the evidence impossible to miss.
4. **Ambitious scale, human clarity.** The ambition should read in the scope and quality of what's built — but the site must remain navigable and legible for a non-technical SME owner making a real decision.
5. **Motion serves the argument.** Animations and interactions exist to direct attention, reveal structure, and build confidence — not to demonstrate technical capability for its own sake.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AAA. All text contrast must meet AAA ratios (7:1 for normal text, 4.5:1 for large). Reduced motion support required — all animations must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Color must never be the only means of conveying information. Custom cursor must not break keyboard or screen reader navigation. Full keyboard navigability. Screen reader semantics on all interactive elements.
